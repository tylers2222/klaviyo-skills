# What Makes a Great Klaviyo Template

Klaviyo's drag-and-drop editor lets marketers build reusable templates that align with their brand and drive conversions. The following best-practice guidance synthesises recommendations from Klaviyo's help center, academy courses and independent design experts. The emphasis is on template structure and components, not the content strategy for individual campaigns.

---

## 1. Core Principles

### 1.1 Maintain Consistent Branding

- **Establish clear brand guidelines** — Klaviyo's academy notes that creating brand guidelines (logo, colour palette, typography, header links and social icons) makes it easier for everyone to build emails that match the brand's style. Upload these assets to Klaviyo's Brand Library so that default templates automatically include them.
- **Consistent header and footer** — Save a header that includes the logo and important links and a footer with social icons, unsubscribe and privacy-policy links. Re-use these blocks so subscribers always know where to find them. Klaviyo allows universal content blocks that can be updated once and reused across templates.
- **Use a master template** — Klaviyo's blog suggests keeping at least one master template so that every email uses the same font sizes for the header, subhead and body text. Different types of emails (newsletter, promotion, transactional) can have their own master templates.
- **Match brand voice** — Use the same tone and style across emails to reinforce your brand identity. The subject line and preview text should reflect your voice and can even be playful to create a "brand moment".

---

## 2. Visual Hierarchy and Layout

Readers often skim rather than read; a good template uses hierarchy to guide the eye:

- **Use headings and subheadings** to break content into digestible sections. Bold headlines can also anchor each section in an inverted pyramid or single-column layout.
- **Allow breathing room** — White space around text and images makes the layout feel clean and improves readability. Set margins and padding consistently using the template's style panel.
- **Highlight the call-to-action (CTA)** — Buttons should stand out with bold colours and adequate contrast so they are easy to find. Klaviyo recommends including a CTA above the fold and using live text and buttons rather than embedding CTAs in images. Limit the number of CTAs and ensure they align with the message.

### 2.1 High-Performing Layouts

| Layout Type | When to Use | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| **Inverted Pyramid** | Digestible editorial emails | Start with a large image to capture attention, add contextual body copy and place the CTA as the focal point. |
| **Single Column** | Simple promotions or announcements | Include one image and a prominent CTA of similar width; a straightforward path guides the reader through the message. |
| **Zigzag** | Featuring multiple products or offers (up to three sections) | Alternate image and text across columns and include a clear CTA in each section; place the most important link first. |

### 2.2 Additional Layout Guidance

- **Balance text and visuals** — Avoid image-only emails. Klaviyo's designers recommend a healthy mix; visuals set the vibe, but copy delivers the message. Heavy images can slow load time and degrade mobile experience.
- **Design for scanners** — Use bullets, short paragraphs and bolded keywords to let readers skim.

---

## 3. Mobile-First & Responsive Design

- **Optimise for mobile** — Over half of email opens occur on mobile. Best practices include a one-column layout, large fonts (14–16 px for body text) and buttons at least 44 px tall. Klaviyo's templates are responsive, but you should preview your design on both desktop and mobile.
- **Use mobile-optimised images** — Images should maintain their impact on small screens. Klaviyo's blog suggests properly sized, mobile-optimised images and subject lines that fit within the mobile inbox preview.
- **Enable mobile optimisation in styles** — In the template's styles panel you can enable mobile optimisation so that sections and blocks adapt for mobile.

---

## 4. Images & Media

- **Use high-quality images judiciously** — Klaviyo recommends including visuals that resonate emotionally and match your brand personality. Images should be between 600 and 1,000 px wide and under 2,000 px tall.
- **File types and sizes** — Klaviyo supports JPEG, PNG and GIF files. PNG files generally display best for images containing text. Images over 1 MB can load slowly; use Klaviyo's built-in compression tool to reduce file sizes. Maintain 72 dpi resolution and keep full-width images under 1,000 px wide to balance quality and loading speed.
- **Alt text** — Always add descriptive alt text because email clients like Outlook and Apple Mail may not display images by default. Alt text also improves accessibility and deliverability.
- **Background images and GIFs** — Background images can add depth to highlight sections. Subtle movement (such as GIFs) can capture attention in a crowded inbox, but keep file sizes small and avoid distracting animations.

---

## 5. Personalisation

- **Profile-based personalisation** — Use customer data (first name, location, birthday, preferences) to make messages feel tailored. Klaviyo's editor provides personalised tags that can be inserted in text fields. Include details like name, email or location to create a "1:1" feel.
- **Custom fields and event-based personalisation** — Build custom tags such as birthday, colour preference or contact preferences to customise messages. Event personalisation uses data from customers' behaviour (items ordered or viewed) to insert dynamic images or recommendations.
- **Segmentation and dynamic blocks** — Klaviyo's show/hide feature can display content based on segment membership. Segment your list by purchased products, funnel stage, geography or zero-party data (survey answers). The show/hide feature allows different banners or promotions to appear depending on the subscriber's reward status.

---

## 6. Accessibility & Readability

- **Alt text and descriptive links** — Add alt text to images, avoid image-only emails and use descriptive links such as "Shop the collection" instead of "Click here". Avoid embedding key information in images and use contrasting colours.
- **Logical structure** — Organise content into a logical hierarchy and use headings to separate ideas. Ensuring a clear reading order helps screen-reader users navigate the email.
- **Contrast & colour choices** — Use starkly contrasting colours (e.g., white on black) and ensure CTAs stand out from the background. The style panel provides a colour picker and the ability to set margins, padding and background colours.
- **Avoid image-only emails** — If images fail to load, subscribers should still understand the message. Always provide live text for key information and CTAs.
- **Design for dark & light modes** — Modern email clients switch between dark and light modes. Ensure your colour palette remains legible in both; Klaviyo's preview tool can display the email as a specific recipient would see it.

---

## 7. CTA & Interactive Elements

- **Single, clear CTA** — Avoid multiple competing CTAs. Keep offers relevant to the season and subject line and use colour to differentiate primary and secondary CTAs.
- **Low-commitment CTAs** — Some readers may be reluctant to click "Buy now." Klaviyo's customer success managers suggest offering low-pressure CTAs like "Learn more" to reduce mental barriers.
- **Buttons vs. image links** — Use live text buttons rather than embedding text in images so you can easily A/B test colours or wording.
- **Place CTAs above the fold** — Make it easy for subscribers to act without scrolling.

---

## 8. Using Klaviyo's Template Editor Effectively

- **Blocks & sections** — Emails are built from blocks (text, images, buttons, tables) organised within sections. Blocks add content; sections group blocks and control their styles. Blocks and sections can be cloned, deleted or moved by dragging.
- **Style hierarchy** — Template-wide styles (background colour, fonts, width and spacing) apply first. Section styles override template styles, and block styles override section styles. Use this hierarchy to set global defaults and customise specific blocks as needed.
- **Universal content** — Save frequently used blocks or sections as "universal content" so you can update them once and have changes propagate to all templates. This is especially useful for headers, footers or product modules.
- **Display options** — Section and block display options let you show or hide content for particular device types or recipient segments.

---

## 9. Putting It All Together

An effective Klaviyo template combines visual hierarchy, mobile-friendly design, consistent branding and personalised content. Designers should start by defining brand styles and creating master templates. During construction, choose a layout that suits the message (inverted pyramid, single column or zigzag) and use blocks to build logical sections. High-quality images, clear CTAs and accessible design ensure the email works across devices and for all recipients. Finally, leverage Klaviyo's powerful personalisation and segmentation tools — dynamic blocks, show/hide features and custom tags — to deliver relevant experiences that drive engagement.
